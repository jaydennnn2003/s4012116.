Week 4 Session 1
During this week the class explored the Black Squares project where students were to create different images/icons to represent certain words through the use of four black squares only.

by completeing multiple iterations of each it allowed us to view all the posible ideas on paper which allowed the designs to evolve to become a closer repesentation to what the criteria for the image asked for. For example, the image for bold started off as four large squares next to and on top of each other, however, after some iterations, it was decided that the image with the one large square with others stacked on top would become the best to represent the word as it demonstrated the idea of bold the best. Below is an example of the work that was produced through this task.
Week 4 Session 2
This week the homework task allocated was to draw up a design of a random icon using at least 20 iterations in 10 minutes to develop a piece that embodies all the great aspects of the previous designs into one, further exploring the task similar to last weeks, however, during this task we were to use a single icon and develop it further.

For this particular task I have chosen an icon that is meant to represent rock climbing. Through brief research and iterations the icon came to an outcome with a design where medium to thick lines were used to express simple yet affective expression, which would be easy on the eye, yet detailed enough to show the viewers what is being presented. Below are the iterations that took place and the process that was taken to reach a sufficient outcome The next activity in this weeks homework was to design at least 5 iterations of the 9 icon assessment, creating a total of 45 sketches total. However, during this weeks homework I created 11 items with 4 sketches each. This was the beginning of the development of the icons to reach a suitable design. Below are the initial developmets of all the designs. For the cloud i believe the fourth design was the best as it is aesthetic, simple and easy to understand. As for the thinking cloud I believe the fourth design was also the best iteration as it best resembles what a thinking cloud looks like. These two designs will go through further iterations on illustrator to make them more suitable for presentation. The pillow was difficult as it needed to incorporate shadow without making the image look too realistic. so as for the pillow I believe the third iteration was the best, the shading of the pillow will be fixed up in illustrator so that it will be able to match the rest of the design as a set. The bubble is a very difficult item to draw as it is transparent having no shadows and only reflections. The third iteration has a nice curve where the reflection is, but like all the other designs will need to be further developed in illustrator. For the moon there were two options to go with; the realistic version or cartoon version. As all the icons follow the simple look I chose to go for the simple crescent. The bed was difficult as it required multiple shapes to demostrate what the object is. In the beginning I tried showing this from an angled point of view but in the end went to the side where it was minimalistic but still determinable. The Swirl above was a random design that indicates the transition into a dream. There were a few iterations as I wasn't sure which way to go at first. But after a few design i made a design which gradually gets smaller as it gets closer to the center. The brain was very difficult to draw as it has so many different aspects and ratios. I began with a simple brain then later a head as well. However, to make the design simple the brain by itself will go forward. The snoring icon, or the depiction of what snoring consists of, three Z's. the simplest design that suites the rest of the icons is the third. The stars are a design that I wasn't too sure about as there was already a moon, however the last design is the simplest and the neetest. there are possibilities that the moon and the stars may join to make one icon The globe went through a few iterations but ultimately stayed the same. The globe struggles to represent the theme dream so it may not be added to the final designs.
Week 5 Session 1
During this weeks homework task we were asked to use hues and saturation to come up with a coloured icon. The initial hues that I decided to go with were light blues and greys to represent dream. In the first image, the cloud, only one hue was used blue, this worked for the cloud as the different value and saturation gave enough contrast for the cloud to stand out. For the second icon, I didn't add another colour but grey was added to give it some more contrast. I wasnt up until the moon and stars when another hue was added. However, I felt the amount of contrast was on the edge of being too much when trying to demonstrate the theme of dream. Any more colour would have made the icon to vibrant and stand out from the other designs.
Week 5 Session 2
This week the homework tasks asked us to continue iterations on the icon designs. This icon design process had been completed last week.
